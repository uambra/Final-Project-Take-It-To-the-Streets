What defenses for specific conditions can be used in court?

Parking and Compliance Tickets

  • Cited vehicle or registration plate (licence plate) were stolen at time of violation
  • Relevant signs saying that parking is restricted were missing
  • Facts alleged in parking or compliance violation are inconsistent and do not suppor that a specfied regulation was violated (e.g., tinted window are used in compliance with a medical use requirement 625 ILCS 5/12-503(g))
  • Parking meter was malfunctioned through no fault of the person recieving the ticket
  • Person was not owner of the cited vehicle at the time of violation

Expired City Sticker and Expired License Plate Violation

  • Illegal vehicle condition was corrected prior to obtaining a hearing
  • The vehicle must be under the name of an individual respondent as a individual or joint owner
  • The defense is applied to the violation that occurred between April 1, 2022 and December 31, 2023, inclusive

Red Light Violations Recorded By Automated Cameras

  • Cited vehicle was an authorized emergency vehicle or was lawfully participating in a funeral procession.
  • Facts alleged for violation are inconsistent or fail to support a finding that the Chicago Municipal code was violated
  • Respondent was not owner of the cited vehicle at the time of the violation
  • Violation occurred when the vehicle or state registration plates (license plates) were reported to a law enforcement agency as having been stolen, and the vehicle/plates have not been recovered by the owner at the time of the violation.

Bus Stop or Bus Lane Violations By Automated Cameras

  • Operator of vehicle was provided with a uniform traffic citation for violation of 9-12-060(b) or a parking violation for 9-64-140(b);
  • Vehicle was an authorized emergency vehicle
  • Facts alleged do not support or are inconsistent with a finding in Section 9-12-060(b) or Section 9-64-140(b)
  • Vehicle, if a licensed taxicab, was stopped to expeditiously load or unload passengers
